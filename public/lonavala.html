Lonavala, Maharashtra

"A Hill Station Paradise"

Lonavala is a picturesque hill station located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, India. Known for its scenic beauty, lush greenery, and cool climate, it attracts tourists year-round. The area is famous for its valleys, waterfalls, and beautiful lakes. The region experiences heavy rainfall during the monsoon, making it a lush green paradise.

Key attractions include the stunning Bushi Dam, the ancient Karla and Bhaja Caves, and the breathtaking views from Lion's Point and Tiger's Leap. Lonavala is also known for its delightful chikki (a traditional Indian sweet) and several hiking trails.

Whether you are seeking adventure, relaxation, or a taste of nature, Lonavala has something for everyone.

Highlights:
  • Bushi Dam: A popular picnic spot with a stunning waterfall.
  • Karla Caves: Ancient rock-cut caves dating back to the 2nd century BCE.
  • Lion's Point: A viewpoint offering panoramic views of the valleys.
  • Chikki: Try the famous sweet delicacy unique to Lonavala.
